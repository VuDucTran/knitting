Color charts and updated retailer lists at www.garnstudio.comKnitting tension: 21 sts x 28 rows on needle size 3.5 mm in pattern = 10 x 10 cm.
Edge: K 3 cm stocking sts, P 1 row (= folding edge), K 3 cm stocking sts. 
Pattern: See diagrams M1-M.4. The diagrams are seen from the RS.  

Back and front piece: The jumper is knitted round on circular needles and cut for armholes and mid front afterwards. Cast on 171-180-189-201-219-231 sts (incl 1 edge st each side towards mid front) on circular needle size 3 mm with charcoal grey  and knit edge = 3 cm stocking sts, P 1 row, 3 cm in M.2 with 1 edge st each side – measure piece from the P row. Change to circular needle size 3.5 mm and charcoal grey, at the same time inc 3 sts mid front (these 3 + 2 edge sts = 5 sts to be cut afterwards. Do not incorporate these sts in pattern but knit them in stocking sts). Continue on M.1, at the same time inc 2-3-4-2-4-2 sts evenly on first round = 171-181-191 (201-221-231) sts + 5 sts for cutting. When piece measures 24-26-29-28-30-31 sts knit next row as follows: 5 sts for cutting, 43-45-48-50-55-58 sts on front piece, cast on 4 sts (to be cut for armhole, knit these sts in stocking sts), 85-91-95-101-111-115 sts for back piece, cast on 4 sts (to be cut for armhole, knit these sts in stocking sts), 43-45-48-50-55-58 sts for front piece. When piece measures 36-39-43-43-46-48 cm cast off the middle 26-26-28-28-30-30 sts + 5 sts for cutting at front for neck and finish the piece back and forth on needle. Dec to shape the neckline at front on every other row: 2 sts 1 time, 1 st 2 times. At the same time when piece measures 38-41-46-46-49-51 cm cast off the middle 29-31-31-33-35-33 sts at back and dec 2 sts each side on next row. Cast off when piece measures 40-43-48-48-51-53 cm. 

Sleeves: Cast on 36-36-39-39-42-42 sts on double pointed needles size 3 mm with charcoal grey and knit edge as described for body piece, at the same time inc 6-10-11-11-10-10 sts on last round = 42-46-50-50-52-52 sts. Change to needle size 3.5 mm and knit M.3 and then M.4. At the same time, after the edge inc 2 sts mid under arm 13-13-15-17-18-20 times as follows: Size 3/4 years: on every 3rd round, size 5/6 – 7/8 - 9/10 years: on every 4th round, size 11/12 – 13/14 years: on every 4th and 5th round alternately = 68-72-80-84-88-92 sts. When piece measures 21-25-28-32-35-38 cm put 4 sts mid under arm on a thread and finish the sleeve back and forth on needle as follows: put 7-7-8-8-9-9 sts on the thread on every other row 3 times. Piece now measures approx 24-28-31-35-37-40 cm. Put all sts back on needle and knit 2 cm round on needle in reverse stocking sts = edge for sewing on sleeve to body pieces. Cast off and knit the other sleeve. 

Assembly: Insert a marking thread in the middle of the inc sts for armhole each side of body piece and mid front. Make 2 seams each side and mid front using your sewing machine, the first seam ½ st from the MT and the other one ½ st from the first one. Cut the piece between these. 
Sew shoulder seams. 
Front edges: Pick up approx 80-86-94-94-102-106 sts along left front piece on needle size 3 mm with charcoal grey and knit edge – see above. Cast off. Fold the edge and attach to WS. Repeat along right front piece, but after 1 cm make 5-5-5-5-6-6 buttonholes evenly distributed. 1 buttonhole = cast off 2 sts and cast on 2 sts on the return row (remember buttonholes on the 3 cm to be folded towards WS). 
Neckline: Pick up approx 86-100 sts round the neck on needle size 3 mm with charcoal grey and knit as follows: 1 row in stocking sts with charcoal grey and continue in M.2 until edge measures 2.5 cm, change to charcoal grey and knit 1 row, purl 1 row (= folding edge), and finish with 3 cm in charcoal grey. Cast off. Turn the edge towards WS and attach. 
Set in sleeves from the RS as follows: sew in the last row before the edge in stocking sts on sleeves to the first row after the seam in the sts for cutting on body pieces. Turn the jumper inside out and sew the piece in stocking sts over the cutting edge on body pieces. Fold all edges and attach. Sew on buttons.   Have you made this or any other of our designs?
