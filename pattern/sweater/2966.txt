Color charts and updated retailer lists at www.garnstudio.comKNITTING TENSION: 10 x 10 cm = 20 sts x 32 rows on needle size 4 mm in moss st.
PATTERN: See diagram. 1 pattern repeat = 50 sts = approx 19 cm.
Moss st: Row 1: * K2, P2 * Row 2: K over K and P over P. Row 3: * P2, K2 * Row 4: Like row 2. Repeat row 1-4. 
RIB: * K1, P1 *

FRONT PIECE: Cast on 72-80 (88-92-100) sts on needle size 2.5 mm and work 5 cm rib, on last row inc evenly to 88-94 (98-102-106) sts. Change to needle size 4 mm and work first row as follows: 19-22 (24-26-28) moss sts, 50 pattern sts - see diagram - and 19-22 (24-26-28) moss sts. Continue in pattern like this. Remember the knitting tension! When piece measures 34-40 (43-48-50) cm cast off the middle 26-28 (28-30-30) sts for neck and complete each shoulder separately. Cast off to shape the neckline at the beg of every row from mid front: 3 sts 1 time, 2 sts 1 time. Cast off remaining 26-28 (30-31-33) sts on shoulder when piece measures 37-43 (47-51-53) cm.

BACK PIECE: Worked in moss st. Cast on 72-80 (88-92-100) sts on needle size 2.5 mm and work 5 cm rib, on last rows inc or dec evenly to 76-82 (86-90-96) sts. Change to needle size 4 mm and continue in moss st until piece measures 37-43 (47-51-53) cm, cast off.

SLEEVE: Cast on 44-48 (48-52-56) sts on needle size 2.5 mm and work 5 cm rib, on last rows inc to 50-50 (54-54-58) sts evenly on needle. Change to needle size 4 mm and continue as follows: pattern on the middle 50 sts with moss st each side. AT THE SAME TIME inc 1 st each side a total of 14-16 (18-22-22) time on every 4th row for size 3/4 and 5/6, and on every 5th row for size 7/8 -9/10 and 11/12 = 78-82 (90-98-102) sts on needle. Work inc sts in moss st. Cast off when sleeve measures 25-29 (35-41-43) cm. 

ASSEMBLY: Sew shoulder seams. Pick up approx 70-80 sts round neck on double pointed needles size 2.5 mm and work 5 rounds stocking st, P 1 round (folding edge), and work 5 rounds stocking st, cast off, fold edge double towards WS and fasten. Set in sleeves and sew sleeve and side seams – make sure seams are even and not too bulky.
